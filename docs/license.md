## What License Does Omega Use?

Omega is released under the **MIT License**, a permissive open-source license that is simple, developer-friendly, and widely used in the software industry.

**In Plain English:** You can use Omega however you want—commercially, privately, in proprietary software, or anything else—as long as you include the original license text and copyright notice.

- **No permission needed** - You don't need to ask omega-cortex for permission to use Omega

### Modify the Code
- **Make changes** - Fork the code, modify it, adapt it to your needs

## What You Must Do

### Include the License
When you distribute Omega (or software based on Omega):

1. **Include the LICENSE file** - Copy the LICENSE file into your distribution
2. **Include the copyright notice** - Keep the line: `Copyright (c) 2026 omega-cortex`
3. **No removal** - Don't remove or hide the license or copyright notice

### Document Changes
- **If distributing modified code** - It's good practice to document what you changed
- **Give credit** - Acknowledge that your work is based on Omega

## What You're NOT Required To Do

- **Release your code as open source** - You can keep modifications private
- **Ask permission** - You don't need omega-cortex's approval
- **Pay money** - The software is free with no fees or royalties
- **Provide support** - You're not obligated to maintain or support the software
